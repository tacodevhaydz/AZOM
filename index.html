Why it exists

MOZA hardware, finally fluent in SimHub.

MOZA makes excellent sim racing hardware — but Pithouse, their companion software, is Windows-only. Linux users had no official way to manage LED effects or stream telemetry to their wheel's dashboard.

  • SimHub runs on Linux via Proton/Wine — this plugin opens MOZA hardware to its entire ecosystem.
  • Map any of the thousands of SimHub data points to your wheel's LCD dashboard.
  • Drive your LEDs through SimHub's full effects pipeline — superb paired with ATSR-EVO.
  • Cross-platform hardware control with first-class, built-in telemetry.

Built on the work of Boxflat, the Linux MOZA control project.

Capabilities

Everything Pithouse does — and everything SimHub adds.

01

SimHub Device Integration

Wheels & dashboards register as native SimHub devices. Drive LEDs through the full Button & Telemetry effects pipeline — RPM, flags, limiter animations, scripted effects. Per-model definitions deploy automatically.

02

LCD Dashboard Telemetry

Stream speed, RPM, gear, lap times, fuel and tyre wear to your wheel's LCD via MOZA's binary telemetry protocol. Auto-detect layouts, hot-reload, per-channel mapping, UTF-8 text fields.

03

Hardware Configuration

Read/write control of wheelbase, wheel, handbrake, pedals and hub — rotation, FFB strength, damping, EQ, output curves. Tabs auto-show based on what's connected.

04

Per-Game Profiles

Every setting is stored per game through SimHub's profile system and switches automatically the moment you launch a different title.

05

AB9 Active Shifter

Full configuration: 5+R / 6+R / 7+R / sequential patterns, resistance, spring, damping and friction feel sliders, plus engine and gear-shift vibration.

06

mBooster Pedal Haptics

Host-rendered pedal effects from live telemetry — ABS pulse, tyre lockup, brake-threshold feedback and RPM-driven continuous vibration, per assigned pedal.

07

Wheelbase Ambient LEDs

R21 / R25 bases expose their 18-LED ambient ring as a dedicated SimHub device — standby animations, sleep mode, brightness and startup colors.

08

Automatic Dashboard Switching

Have your dashboard automatically load with the game. Even supports per-car profiles for even greater customization.

Get started

Installed in four steps.

  1. Download the release

    Grab the latest MozaPlugin_<version>.zip from the Releases page.

  2. Drop in the DLL

    Extract MozaPlugin.dll into your SimHub directory — defaults to C:\Program Files (x86)\SimHub\.

  3. Restart SimHub

    The plugin appears under Settings › Plugins as “MOZA Control”.

  4. Connect & detect

    Plug in your hardware and restart once more. Devices are auto-detected, definitions deployed — then add them under Devices. Requires SimHub 9.11.8+.

Close Pithouse first

Both apps talk to MOZA hardware over the same serial port and can't run together. Pithouse must be fully closed — not just minimized — before SimHub can connect.
